Through dozens of Critical Conversation events this year, Middlebury is demonstrating its commitment to free expression and inclusiveness on our campuses, in our classrooms, and throughout our diverse intellectual community.

  • Perhaps more than anything, Americans need to learn to talk to one another again.

    We are becoming a nation at odds with its political and social traditions—traditions of intense interest in ideas, in conversation, in listening and debating, and in sustaining arguments that persuade and move people. Our nation was founded in argument. Our colleges and universities are places dedicated to free inquiry and free expression. Our obligation to uphold those principles is unique. The same is true for our obligation to bring into the discussion more people for whom access to the public square has been limited. Equality of access is essential for freedom of expression to find its highest purpose.

  • Launched in the fall of 2017, “Critical Conversations: Advancing a culture of freedom and inclusivity” is designed to bring together and focus the attention of the Middlebury College community on pressing issues of public interest, including the question of free expression and inclusivity in higher education and society at large. During this academic year, Middlebury will host many events that we hope will spur discussion and debate. Some of these are the types of events seen on campus every year. Others are being produced this year expressly to fulfill this commitment. Through Critical Conversations, we hope to create a truly robust and inclusive public sphere at Middlebury that encourages engagement with ideas and issues that are important to our times and to our mission as an institution of higher education.
